# Bible Theme Exegesis Report Template

Use this template when the user asks for a formal Bible theme study, especially one that should become a Word/PDF report.

## Title Page

- Report title
- Biblical book or passage range
- Theme
- Main research question
- Source base
- Date

## Executive Summary

State the report's core conclusion in one paragraph.

Then list 5-8 major findings:

- Textual finding
- Greek or syntactic finding
- Theological finding
- Commentary finding
- Pastoral or teaching implication

## 1. Research Question and Scope

Include:

- Primary question
- Sub-questions
- Passage or book scope
- Inclusion and exclusion criteria
- Limits of the study

## 2. Research Method

Use this order:

1. Corpus inventory
2. Greek keyword search
3. Occurrence classification
4. Syntax and discourse observation
5. Translation comparison, including NASB and Chinese
6. Commentary engagement
7. Theological synthesis
8. Pastoral or teaching application

## 3. Occurrence Map

Use a table:

| Reference | Greek Expression | NASB Rendering | Chinese Translation | Category | Initial Judgment |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
|  |  |  |  |  |  |

Categories may include:

- Direct theme reference
- Related concept
- Human spirit or inner life
- Figurative or non-theme use
- Disputed use

## 4. Book-Level Argument

Explain where the theme appears in the book's argument.

Include:

- Major book divisions
- How earlier passages prepare for later passages
- How the theme develops
- Whether there is a climax passage
- How later ethical, pastoral, or missional sections apply the theme

## 5. Key Passage Exegesis

For every major passage, use this structure:

### Reference and Theme Function

Explain why the passage matters for the theme.

### Greek Text Unit

Quote only the needed phrase, clause, or short verse segment.

### Greek-NASB-Chinese Observation

Use the observation pattern in `greek-nasb-chinese-table.md`.

### Context

Explain immediate and book-level context.

### Commentary Dialogue

Summarize relevant commentators. Separate:

- What the commentator argues
- Why it matters
- Whether the report agrees, qualifies, or rejects it

### Exegetical Conclusion

State the passage's contribution to the theme.

## 6. Theological Synthesis

Organize synthesis by the text's own development, not by an imposed system.

Possible synthesis categories:

- Relation to God the Father
- Relation to Christ
- Relation to salvation
- Relation to covenant or people of God
- Relation to holiness and obedience
- Relation to suffering, hope, and eschatology
- Relation to church life and mission

## 7. Comparison With Other Biblical Books

Use only when helpful.

Compare:

- Vocabulary
- Narrative or argument role
- Theological emphasis
- Continuity and difference

## 8. Pastoral, Teaching, or Sermon Application

Application should arise from exegesis.

Include:

- Doctrinal clarity
- Spiritual formation
- Church life
- Prayer and worship
- Mission and service
- Warnings against misreadings

## 9. Conclusion

Restate the central claim and summarize how the theme functions in the book.

## Appendices

Possible appendices:

- Search terms
- Full occurrence table
- Greek parsing table
- Commentary bibliography
- Reusable sermon or lesson outline

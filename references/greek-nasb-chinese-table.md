# Greek-NASB-Chinese Observation Pattern

Use this pattern whenever a report analyzes a Greek word, phrase, clause, or important syntactic unit.

## Required Order

1. Greek Text
2. Lemma and Parsing
3. Syntax
4. Semantic Range in Context
5. NASB Rendering and Explanation
6. Chinese Translation
7. Exegetical Significance

## Paragraph Format

Use this format for major passages where a table would be too compressed:

### Greek Text

Quote the relevant Greek word, phrase, or short clause.

### Lemma and Parsing

Identify:

- Lemma
- Form
- Part of speech
- Case, number, gender, tense, voice, mood, person, where relevant

### Syntax

Explain the unit's syntactic role:

- Subject, object, modifier, prepositional phrase, genitive relation, participial function, infinitival purpose, discourse marker, or other role

### Semantic Range in Context

Explain the likely meaning in this passage.

Do not list dictionary meanings without deciding which meaning fits the context.

### NASB Rendering and Explanation

Give the NASB rendering of the relevant phrase if available.

Then explain:

- What Greek element the NASB preserves clearly
- What interpretive decision the NASB makes
- Whether the NASB wording clarifies or obscures the Greek structure

Keep NASB quotation short. Do not reproduce long passages.

### Chinese Translation

Give a faithful Chinese translation of the Greek unit.

When helpful, compare:

- Personal translation
- User's preferred Chinese Bible
- A more literal rendering
- A more readable teaching rendering

### Exegetical Significance

State how this Greek observation affects the passage's meaning and the report's theme.

## Example

Reference: Romans 5:5

Greek Text: `διὰ πνεύματος ἁγίου`

Lemma and Parsing: `πνεῦμα`, genitive singular neuter after `διά`; `ἅγιος`, genitive singular neuter modifying `πνεύματος`.

Syntax: Instrumental prepositional phrase; the Spirit is the means by which God's love is poured into believers' hearts.

NASB Rendering and Explanation: NASB renders the phrase as “through the Holy Spirit.” This preserves the instrumental force of `διά` and identifies the Spirit as the agent through whom God communicates his love.

Chinese Translation: “借着圣灵” or “藉着圣灵”。The phrase emphasizes means rather than merely location or atmosphere.

Exegetical Significance: The love of God in Romans 5 is not only a doctrine inferred from the cross; it is also inwardly applied to believers by the Holy Spirit.
